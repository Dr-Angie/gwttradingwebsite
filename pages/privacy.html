Privacy Policy

GWT Trading Pty Ltd

1. Website Privacy Notice

GWT Trading Pty Ltd ("GWT", "we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and confidentiality of personal information collected through our website, business operations, and service delivery activities.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, hold, use, disclose, and protect personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

2. Scope

This policy applies to personal information collected from:

  • Website visitors
  • Customers and end users
  • Business partners and suppliers
  • Contractors and subcontractors
  • Employment candidates and referees

This policy does not apply to employee records, which are handled in accordance with applicable workplace laws.

3. What Is Personal Information?

Personal information is information or an opinion about an identified individual, or an individual who is reasonably identifiable, whether the information is true or not and whether recorded in material form or not.

Sensitive information includes (but is not limited to) health information, criminal records, union membership, or other information defined as sensitive under the Privacy Act. Sensitive information is only collected with consent or where required or authorised by law.

4. Information We Collect

The type of personal information we collect depends on your relationship with GWT and may include:

  • Identity and contact details (such as name, email address, phone number, and postal address)
  • Business and commercial information (such as company name, role, billing and payment details)
  • Website enquiry and communication data
  • Recruitment-related information (such as resumes, qualifications, and references)

Website and Digital Data

When you visit our website, we may collect limited technical information such as IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, referring websites, and usage data. This information is used to improve website performance, security, and user experience and does not usually identify you personally.

5. How We Collect Personal Information

We may collect personal information in a number of lawful and fair ways, including:

  • Through our website and online contact forms
  • Via email, telephone, or written correspondence
  • During business transactions and service delivery
  • Through recruitment and onboarding processes
  • From publicly available sources (where lawful)
  • Through secure cloud-based business systems

6. Purposes for Which We Collect and Use Personal Information

We collect, hold, use, and disclose personal information where it is reasonably necessary for our business activities, including:

  • Delivering services and managing projects
  • Customer, partner, and supplier relationship management
  • Asset logistics, commissioning, and service coordination
  • Recruitment and contractor engagement
  • Business administration, invoicing, and compliance
  • Improving our services, systems, and website

We may also collect, use, or disclose personal information where required or authorised by law, or where you have provided consent.

7. Direct Marketing

We may use personal information (excluding sensitive information) to provide information about our services, updates, or business activities.

You may opt out of receiving marketing communications at any time by contacting us or using any unsubscribe option provided in the communication.

8. Disclosure of Personal Information

We may disclose personal information:

  • Internally within GWT Trading Pty Ltd
  • To trusted service providers, contractors, or partners engaged to support our operations
  • Where required by law, regulation, or court order

We take reasonable steps to ensure that third parties handle personal information in a manner consistent with the Australian Privacy Principles.

Overseas Disclosure

Some personal information may be stored or processed using secure cloud-based systems. These systems may involve servers or support services located outside Australia. Where this occurs, GWT takes reasonable steps to ensure overseas recipients comply with Australian privacy obligations.

9. Data Security and Retention

We take reasonable steps to protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss, unauthorised access, modification, or disclosure.

If personal information is no longer required for any lawful purpose and we are not legally required to retain it, we will take reasonable steps to securely destroy or de-identify the information.

10. Access to Personal Information

You may request access to personal information that we hold about you by contacting our Privacy Officer. Requests will be responded to within a reasonable period and in accordance with the Privacy Act.

In certain circumstances, access may be refused. If this occurs, we will provide written reasons and information about available complaint mechanisms.

11. Correction of Personal Information

If you believe personal information we hold about you is inaccurate, out of date, incomplete, irrelevant, or misleading, you may request correction by contacting our Privacy Officer.

We will take reasonable steps to correct the information and respond within a reasonable timeframe. If we refuse a correction request, we will provide written reasons and information about complaint options.

12. Data Breaches

If we become aware of a data breach that is likely to result in serious harm to individuals, we will respond in accordance with the Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) scheme, including notifying affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner where required.

13. Anonymity and Pseudonymity

Where practicable, you may interact with GWT anonymously or using a pseudonym. However, in some circumstances this may limit our ability to provide services or respond to enquiries.

14. Complaints

If you believe we have breached the Australian Privacy Principles, you may lodge a complaint by contacting our Privacy Officer. We will investigate and respond within a reasonable period.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may refer your complaint to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).
